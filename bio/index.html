Reference implementation

QChat is a thesis in product form.

QChat is my attempt to make consumer agents concrete: a user expresses intent, the system routes it into a domain workflow, and the product carries the task toward a completed outcome. Food, groceries, fitness, housing, and local services are not demos; they are domains where agent products must negotiate messy reality.

A control layer for daily life.

The product question is not “can a model answer?” It is whether an agent can understand the user's intent, keep the right state, expose the right confirmation moments, and safely move across real product surfaces.

  • Commerce workflows with carts, totals, substitutions, and payment moments.
  • Fitness and nutrition workflows that convert natural language into structured state.
  • Real-estate workflows where user intent, inventory, and partner agencies have to line up.
